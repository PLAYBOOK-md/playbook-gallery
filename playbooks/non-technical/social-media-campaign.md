# Social Media Campaign Builder

Plan and draft a complete social media campaign, from audience research to ready-to-post content.

This playbook is built for marketers, creators, and small business owners who need to go from "I have a product" to "I have a week of posts ready" without hiring an agency. It adapts to the platform and tone you choose, producing content you can copy-paste into your scheduler.

## SYSTEM

You are a social media strategist and copywriter. You understand platform-specific best practices, write scroll-stopping hooks, and know how to balance promotion with value. Always write content that sounds human, not like an ad.

## INPUTS

- `product` (string): The product or service to promote
- `audience` (text): Description of the target audience
- `platforms` (enum: Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, TikTok): Primary platform for this campaign
- `tone` (enum: professional, casual, playful): Voice and tone for the content

## STEP 1: Research Audience and Platform

Analyze the target audience and platform for this campaign:

**Product:** {{product}}
**Audience:** {{audience}}
**Platform:** {{platforms}}
**Tone:** {{tone}}

Research and outline:
1. What this audience cares about and what problems they face
2. What type of content performs best on {{platforms}} right now
3. Optimal posting times and frequency for {{platforms}}
4. 5 relevant hashtags or keywords this audience follows
5. What competitors or similar brands are doing well on this platform

## STEP 2: Create Content Calendar

Build a 5-day content calendar for promoting {{product}} on {{platforms}}.

For each day, specify:
- **Day and theme** (e.g., Monday — Problem Awareness)
- **Content format** (e.g., carousel, short video script, text post, story)
- **Hook** — the first line or visual concept that stops the scroll
- **Goal** — what this post should accomplish (awareness, engagement, conversion)

Mix up the formats. Not every post should be a hard sell — follow the 80/20 rule (80% value, 20% promotion).

## STEP 3: Draft Posts

Write all 5 posts for the {{product}} campaign in a {{tone}} tone.

For each post include:
1. **The full caption or script** — ready to copy and paste
2. **Hashtags** — 5-10 relevant hashtags for {{platforms}}
3. **Visual direction** — a one-sentence description of the image, graphic, or video to pair with it
4. **Best time to post** — based on {{platforms}} best practices

Make sure the posts tell a story across the week. Each one should stand alone, but together they should build momentum toward a clear call to action.

## STEP 4: Write Engagement Templates

Create a set of response templates for engaging with people who interact with the {{product}} campaign on {{platforms}}.

Write templates for:
- **Positive comments** — thank them and deepen the conversation
- **Questions about the product** — answer helpfully without being salesy
- **Skeptical or negative comments** — respond gracefully and professionally
- **DM inquiries** — a warm first reply that moves toward a conversion
- **Comment prompts** — 3 questions you can post to boost engagement on your own content

Keep everything in a {{tone}} voice. These should feel natural, not scripted.
